IT Ethics

Technology professionals have a responsibility to build systems that are useful, secure, fair, and respectful of users.

Ethical Guidelines for Developers

Developers should think beyond whether a system simply works. They should also consider whether the system is safe, honest, fair, and respectful of people. The ACM Code of Ethics explains that computing professionals should contribute to society, avoid harm, be honest and trustworthy, be fair, respect privacy, and honor confidentiality.

In practice, this means developers should avoid creating misleading software, should test their work carefully, should be honest about limitations, and should protect user information. A website, database, app, or AI tool should be built in a way that reduces harm and respects the people who use it.

Key Responsibilities

  • Protect user privacy and confidential information.
  • Be honest about what technology can and cannot do.
  • Avoid harm by testing systems and fixing known problems.
  • Design systems that are fair and do not discriminate.
  • Follow laws, policies, and professional standards.

Ethical Considerations for Data and AI

Privacy

Data professionals should only collect and use data for legitimate purposes. Sensitive information should be protected with strong security practices and should not be shared without proper permission.

Fairness

Data and AI systems can create unfair results if the data is biased or incomplete. Professionals should review data sources, test outcomes, and consider whether a system treats people fairly.

Accountability

Organizations should be able to explain how important systems are designed, tested, and monitored. The NIST AI Risk Management Framework encourages organizations to manage AI risks and improve trustworthiness.

My Reflection

IT ethics matters because technology is connected to real people. A weak password system, careless database, biased algorithm, or poorly tested application can affect privacy, safety, and trust. As an IT student with an interest in cybersecurity, I think ethical decision-making should be part of every stage of technology work, from planning and design to testing, deployment, and maintenance.
